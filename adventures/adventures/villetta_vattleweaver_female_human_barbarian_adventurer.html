Key Aspects of a DnD5e Barbarian

Rage

A barbarian can fly into a rage a certain number of times per day, increasing their strength, constitution, and melee damage output.

Rules: A barbarian can use the Rage ability twice per long rest, starting at level 1. While raging, they gain advantage on Strength checks and saving throws, take half damage from physical attacks, and deal an extra 2 damage with melee weapon attacks. Rage lasts for 1 minute, ending early if the barbarian is knocked unconscious, their turn ends and they haven't attacked a hostile creature since their last turn, or if they choose to end it early as a bonus action.

Unarmored Defense

A barbarian gains additional armor class while not wearing armor or using a shield.

Rules: At level 1, a barbarian's AC is equal to 10 + their Dexterity modifier + their Constitution modifier, while not wearing armor or using a shield. This feature improves as they level up.

Brutal Critical

A barbarian's critical hits with melee attacks deal additional damage.

Rules: Starting at level 9, when a barbarian scores a critical hit with a melee weapon attack, they roll one additional weapon damage die and add it to the extra damage of the critical hit. This feature improves at higher levels.

Reckless Attack

A barbarian can choose to make their attacks with advantage, but also becomes vulnerable to attacks until the start of their next turn.

Rules: Starting at level 2, a barbarian can choose to make their melee weapon attacks with advantage on their turn, but all attacks against them have advantage until the start of their next turn. This feature improves at higher levels.

Totem Spirit

A barbarian can choose a totem animal spirit, granting them additional abilities and bonuses.

Rules: Starting at level 3, a barbarian can choose a totem animal spirit from the options of bear, eagle, or wolf. Each spirit grants the barbarian additional abilities and bonuses while raging. This feature improves at higher levels.

Barbarian Training Lore
The history of barbarian training can be traced back to the earliest days of civilization. In many cultures, young warriors were trained from an early age to hone their fighting skills and become fierce defenders of their tribe or clan.
The training methods of barbarians varied from culture to culture, but they all shared a common emphasis on physical strength and endurance. Often, young warriors were put through grueling physical tests and forced to endure extreme hardships to toughen them up for battle.
In some cultures, barbarians were revered for their strength and bravery, and their training was considered a sacred duty. In others, they were viewed as savage and uncivilized, and their training was seen as a necessary evil to protect the community from outside threats.
As civilization progressed and technology advanced, the role of the barbarian changed. While they were once the primary defenders of their people, they now found themselves in a world where their skills were less in demand. Many turned to mercenary work, selling their services as fighters for hire to the highest bidder.
Despite their changing role, barbarian training remained an important part of many cultures. Even in the modern world, where magic and technology have changed the face of warfare, there are still those who believe that the old ways of the barbarian hold value and should be preserved.

Despite being content with her position in the guild, Villetta longed for adventure in the wilds. She admired the barbarian training and wanted to experience it for herself. Villetta knew that there were risks to being a teamster, but she also knew that the rewards were worth it. She was proud of the economic value that her guild brought to the town, and she was excited to continue leading them into the future.

Villetta's Encounter with a Giant
Villetta was on the road, leading a caravan of wagons filled with grain, with her husband Jacquelin and son Knox by her side. Suddenly, a giant appeared on the path ahead, towering over them and looking hungry.
Knox, being a druid, started to talk to the giant, telling it about all the good mushrooms in the area that it could eat instead of attacking them. Surprisingly, the giant seemed to be listening and was even starting to warm up to Knox.

Avoiding Combat

Deception

A player might attempt to deceive the enemy by pretending to be a member of their faction or by misleading them about their intentions. The DM will typically call for a Charisma (Deception) check and determine the outcome based on the player's roll and the enemy's insight.

Persuasion

A player might attempt to persuade the enemy to stand down or to negotiate a peaceful resolution. The DM will typically call for a Charisma (Persuasion) check and determine the outcome based on the player's roll and the enemy's disposition.

Intimidation

A player might attempt to intimidate the enemy into backing down or surrendering. The DM will typically call for a Charisma (Intimidation) check and determine the outcome based on the player's roll and the enemy's resolve.

Stealth

A player might attempt to sneak past the enemy or to use stealth to avoid being detected. The DM will typically call for a Dexterity (Stealth) check and determine the outcome based on the player's roll and the enemy's perception.

Stopping Combat Options

Surrender

If the players find themselves outnumbered or outmatched in combat, they may choose to surrender. Surrendering means they give up the fight and are at the mercy of their enemies. Depending on the situation, the enemies may take them prisoner, rob them, or let them go.

Negotiate

If the players have a specific goal they are trying to achieve in the encounter, they may choose to negotiate with their enemies instead of fighting. Negotiation could involve offering something in exchange for what they want, such as gold or information, or it could involve appealing to the enemies' sense of reason or compassion.

Use Diplomacy or Persuasion

If the players are dealing with enemies who are not outright hostile, they may be able to use diplomacy or persuasion to avoid combat. This could involve convincing the enemies that they are not a threat, or appealing to their sense of honor or duty to find a non-violent solution to the conflict.

Use Intimidation or Deception

Alternatively, the players may try to use intimidation or deception to end the combat before it starts. This could involve bluffing their way out of a fight, convincing the enemies that they are much more powerful than they really are, or using fear to drive them off.

Retreat

If all else fails, the players may choose to retreat from the encounter. This could involve running away, hiding, or using magic or other abilities to create a distraction and make their escape. Depending on the situation, the enemies may pursue them or let them go.

The Basics of DND5 Combat with a Twist

Picture this, brave adventurers, a world of magic, danger, and excitement. You find yourself in the heat of battle, and the clock is ticking. Every move you make counts. You have one minute to play your action card or write down your action. Time is precious, and the stakes are high. Will you succeed?

Remember, everyone plays their action card or writes down their action in secret. The DM may pause the timer, but try to keep the adrenaline pumping. Once the timer is up, or everyone has finished, roll all the dice you need for your action. Take note of what rolls you would use in different conditions. This is where the real fun begins.

Everyone rolls dice to hit, deal damage, and make three ordered saving throws to be used in the order of action. Everything happens at the same time, all players and the DM. Then, evaluate everything in the order of initiative, and it can't be changed. Reactions must be played before the next action is evaluated. Tap twice on the table to show you're ready for the next round.

But remember, if you don't make it in time, no action. So, be brave, be bold, and make every move count!

Combat: Barbarian vs Giant Spider

A first level barbarian, Grond, comes face to face with a giant spider in a dark dungeon. The spider is perched on a web, ready to strike.

Grond's stats:

  • Level: 1
  • Race: Half-Orc
  • Class: Barbarian
  • Hit Points: 12
  • Armor Class: 14 (Hide Armor)
  • Speed: 30 ft.

Grond's Weapons:

  • Greataxe: 1d12 slashing damage, +4 to hit
  • Handaxe: 1d6 slashing damage, +4 to hit
  • Javelin: 1d6 piercing damage, range 30/120, +4 to hit

The giant spider is a fearsome opponent, with sharp fangs and a venomous bite.

Giant Spider's stats:

  • Hit Points: 11
  • Armor Class: 14 (Natural Armor)
  • Speed: 30 ft., climb 30 ft.

Giant Spider's Abilities:

  • Bite: 1d8 piercing damage, DC 11 Constitution save or take 2d8 poison damage
  • Web: Range 30 ft., DC 11 Dexterity save or be restrained until freed

The combat begins!

Round 1:

Grond rolls a 15 for initiative and goes first. He charges at the spider with his greataxe and rolls a 22 to hit. He deals 9 points of slashing damage to the spider.

The spider retaliates with a bite attack, rolling a 16 to hit. Grond fails his Constitution save and takes 8 points of piercing damage and 5 points of poison damage.

Round 2:

Grond swings his greataxe again, rolling a 10 to hit. The axe glances off the spider's armor, doing no damage.

The spider tries to bite Grond again, rolling a 14 to hit. Grond succeeds on his Constitution save, taking only 3 points of piercing damage.

Round 3:

Grond takes another swing with his greataxe, but this time the spider dodges out of the way, scurrying up the wall and out of reach.
The spider bites back at Grond, but misses as the barbarian quickly steps to the side.
Grond remembers that he was told the spider is worth more alive for its silk, so he tries to grapple it. He rushes forward, attempting to grab onto the spider's legs and restrain it.
The spider fights back, trying to break free from Grond's grasp.

Round 4:

Grond manages to grab onto the spider's legs, restraining it and preventing it from moving away.
The spider tries to break free from Grond's grasp, but the barbarian's grip is too strong.
Grond's Greataxe is too unwieldy to use in this situation, so he instead tries to punch the spider with his fist.
